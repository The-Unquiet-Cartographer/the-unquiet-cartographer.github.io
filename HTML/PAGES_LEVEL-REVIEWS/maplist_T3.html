Map Rating Tags Review
Basin ***'
The key features of this twobase are the titular basin in the center and the cave-like passageways.
Perfect Circle **'
Circular 3-tier arena. The central tower provides engagements at varying ranges but the stairways are cumbersome.
Humper **'
Twobase with a pool in the central chamber and a water passage linking the areas.
Over & Under ***
This fairly standard twobase makes for quite a good free-for-all map with it's multiple levels and basement area.
Spasm ***
This twobase is cramped. The routes to the bases provide no differentiation. The teleporters lead to opposite bases.
ICU ***'
Interesting twobase where the bases sit side-by-side. Windows grant vision from one base to the other and from the bases into the central area.
Ladders & Ledges ***
Twobase with an interesting three-tier layout. Notable as a CTF maps.
Overlook **'
Looks like the inside of a rusty shipping container; a symmetrical and very open twobase.
Where are you? ***'
One of the few asymmetric layouts, this map has three rooms and is notable for its high quantity of powerups and strategic teleporter.
Springboard ****
A symmetrical two base with two floors, the main features are the jump pads in the center which boost you towards the opposing base.
Giblet ****
An old favorite. A rusty-brown bunker with an underground tunnel network. Excellent 2-3 player games.
Pop The Weasel ***'
Small with a deceptively simple layout and strong circular flow.
Metal Keep ****
This map is symmetrical widthways and makes for an excellent duel map.
Altar **'
A really boring twobase.
Paint the Walls ***
Another map with widthways symmetry, this map is longer than it is wide and has several floors.
Baron's Grounds ***
A mostly symmetrical deathmatch map; this level is too small for larger games and could do with a third main area. Nice geometry.
Who is it? ***'
A very vertical map that's a little too chaotic with more than two players, but quite good in 1v1s.
Shipping & Recieving ****
An utterly frantic arena with, paradoxically, both plenty of cover and no places to hide. The strategic interplay between the exposed upper ring and the cramped inner area is excellent, especially when you factor in the powerups which force you to climb up and then drop down again to collect them.
Jump for It *'
An arena divided into an upper and lower area, with a severe deficiency in weapons. Games seem to devolve into running circuits between the dropdown on the top level and the teleporters on the bottom, usually with no ammo to speak of. Terrible.
As Above So Below ****'
A relatively large and complex level with nominal asymmetry, excellent verticality and near-perfect item placement.
The Outer Rim ****'
A large and asymmetrical map with an open center and a great mix of ranges and vertical elements.
Pool Party ****
This semi-symmetrical map doesn't look like much, but it plays suprisingly well, and it's (suprisingly) one of the levels that sticks out in my memory.
The Catwalks ***
A concrete structure sunk in a deep cavern; reminds me of a Red Faction map. It's a shame that there is altogether too much running around and not enough action.
Tier Drop ***
Asymmetric but kinda boring.
Watervator ***'
Asymmetric climb built around a titular watervator.
