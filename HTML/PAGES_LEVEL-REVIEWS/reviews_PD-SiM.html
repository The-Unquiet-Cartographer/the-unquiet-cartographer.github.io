Name
Carrington Villa

Always one of my favourite campaign levels with its sunny atmosphere and intricate layout, what makes this multiplayer treatment effective is the asymmetric balance between the two ends of the map. The weapon 6 slot spawns at one end, while the 2 & 4 slots are weighted towards the other.

Making the 6 slot a contestable item like a Shield is a great way to go in FFA; the weapon positioning forces players to explore the map and downplays camping. In TDM I would place the Shield in 4 and a power weapon like the Crossbow or Reaper in 6 to create more contestable hotspots. The Slayer would be a good choice if the Bots weren't so stupid using it.

The map looks astonishing - it's easily one of the prettiest locations in an N64 game - just don't look at the sky; it's conspicuously absent to improve performance. One gripe I have is the inclusion of the physics objects - these get in the way of movement and can easily be shoved up against doorways, often to fatal consequences.

There are also locations in this level which are sadly underutilised - the courtyards and bedrooms for one - but mainly the cliffside observatory and cave system. It's inclusion is nice, but you will likely never see it in a match, which is okay because the level is sprawling enough as it is.

Air Base

This level can be fun, but it can also get campy in the middle, where weapons 4, 5, & 6 are all fairly close together. The two weapon 5 slots are also right next to each other, and Disabling a slot doesn't seem to make a difference. I think I would have prefered a "frontzone" version of this level - cut off the hangar area with the slow-ass elevators; it really kills the flow.

Name
Attack Ship

They left out most of the map on this one (although if you get a bugged spawn you can explore the rest of the level - just drop down opposite the T-Junction!). Although the weapon spawns seem a bit arbitrary, this level works well in team games, where the reactor area becomes the main "base" to be contested.

In FFA a power weapon like the Mauler is more appropriate for slots 5+6; in TDM this territory is the main corridor for pushing enemy territory and a general support weapon - the Falcon or MagSec - is more appropriate.

Name
Skedar Ruins

Suprisingly good level. Very linear, but players are directed towards the outdoor area and the weapon layout feels appropriate. Better than Air Base or Air Force 1. Good for team games too, although bots do tend to get stuck :( Maybe swap the shield and K7 for team games, so the shields are closer to the middle and therefore more contested?
